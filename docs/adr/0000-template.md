## Context

What forced a decision here? State the problem, the constraints that are real
(measured numbers, platform limits, budget), and what we knew at the time.

If a measurement drove this, put the number in. "Capture was 45% of the recorder
profile" is context; "capture felt slow" is not.

## Decision

What we are doing, in one or two sentences, in the active voice.

> We will store full keyframes every N events and invertible deltas between them.

## Alternatives considered

The heart of the document. For each option we did not take:

### <Alternative A>

What it was, what it would have bought us, and **the specific reason we rejected
it**. "Too complex" is not a reason. "Costs a week plus a permanent three-OS
build matrix for a 22% win capped by Amdahl's law" is a reason.

## Consequences

Honest, both directions.

**What this buys us:** ...

**What this costs us:** ... (every real decision costs something; an ADR with no
costs listed is marketing)

**What this forces later:** the decisions this one now constrains.

**Reversal trigger:** the condition under which we would revisit this. If there
genuinely isn't one, say so and explain why the decision is permanent.
